About This Experience

Skip the hiking boots and see Banff from the water. The Bow River Raft Float is a self-guided, relaxed float down one of the most scenic stretches of river in the Canadian Rockies. No whitewater, no guide barking instructions — just you, your group, and the mountains.

The route follows a calm, gentle section of the Bow River as it winds through Banff National Park. You'll float past towering peaks, through forested river valleys, and alongside wildlife habitat. The current does most of the work — paddle when you feel like it, drift when you don't.

We set you up with everything you need: a high-quality inflatable raft, paddles, Transport Canada-approved life jackets, and a dry bag to keep your phone and valuables safe. Before you launch, our team gives you a quick safety briefing and rundown of the route, including where to pull out at the end.

When you reach the take-out point, our shuttle picks you up and drives you back to where you started. The whole experience takes about 2 to 3 hours depending on how long you linger on the water. It's the easiest way to spend a summer afternoon in Banff.

How It Works

Step 1
Check In at the Launch Point
Arrive at our launch site on the Bow River. Check in, grab your gear, and meet our team. We'll get your raft ready and sized life jackets for everyone in your group.
Step 2
Safety Briefing
Our team walks you through basic paddling technique, the route ahead, where to watch for shallow sections, and exactly where to pull out at the end. Takes about 10 minutes.
Step 3
Launch and Float
Push off and let the Bow River carry you. The current is gentle and steady — paddle to steer, or just drift and take in the mountain scenery. Stop on a gravel bar if you feel like it.
Step 4
Take Out
When you reach the marked take-out point, pull your raft ashore. Our team is there to help you out of the water and collect the equipment.
Step 5
Shuttle Back
Hop on our shuttle for a quick ride back to the launch point and your vehicle. The whole experience takes about 2 to 3 hours, depending on how long you spend on the water.
